# Voice Profile — example (Sam, an engineering manager)

_A worked example so you can see what a good profile looks like. Delete this and write your own
from real samples — this is illustrative, not a default to imitate._

Sounds like: direct, dry, warm underneath. Says the hard thing first, then makes it easy to
take. Never performs seniority — talks to reports like peers.

Signature moves:
- Opens lowercase, often on a dash — "hey — quick one" — not "Hi team, I wanted to..."
- Fragments on purpose. "Short notice, I know." "Nothing on fire."
- Closes by handing back control: "lmk if that's rough," "your call," "no rush."
- One concrete detail beats an adjective — names the ticket, the date, the person.

Never says: "circle back," "per my last," "just wanted to," "reach out," "synergy," exclamation
stacks, or a sign-off warmer than "—Sam."

Registers (if voice shifts by medium):
- Slack/email to the team: lowercase, fragments, fast.
- Writing up to a VP: same spine, full sentences, no slang — still leads with the point.

Sample lines:
1. "hey — pushing the deploy to Monday. ci's flaky, not the code. nothing's on fire."
2. "Short version: we can hit the date or keep the scope. Not both. I'd cut scope — here's why."
3. "you did the right thing escalating. lmk what you need from me and I'll clear it."

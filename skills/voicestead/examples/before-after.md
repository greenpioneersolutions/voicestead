# Before / After Gallery

Worked examples across modes and formats. Each shows the raw input, the delivered output, and the moves — the same "show your hand" the skill performs live.

## 1. Draft — the email nudge
**Raw:** "hey just wanted to reach out and see if you had any updates on the timeline thing we discussed, no rush but would be great to know when you get a chance"
**Delivered:** "Quick check on the timeline we talked about — where does it stand? No rush, whenever you get a chance."
**Moves:** point first, throat-clearing cut, warmth kept.

## 2. Draft — the internal push
**Raw:** "I think it would probably be a good idea for us to consider possibly moving to the new system because it could potentially help with some of our efficiency issues."
**Delivered:** "We should move to the new system. It fixes the two things slowing us down most: [bottleneck A] and [bottleneck B]."
**Moves:** claim first, compulsive hedges cut, so-what chain run until "efficiency issues" became real bottlenecks — left as placeholders rather than invented.

## 3. Rhythm repair — breaking the triad
**Raw:** "The new system is faster, cheaper, and simpler. It saves time, cuts errors, and lifts morale."
**Delivered:** "The new system is faster and cheaper. It also saves time — and, more to the point, people stopped dreading Monday's report."
**Moves:** two parallel triads became a pair, a short clause, and one human detail. The metronome breaks.

## 4. Zombie-noun thaw
**Raw:** "The implementation of the new review process resulted in the reduction of rework and the improvement of code quality."
**Delivered:** "Since we started the new review process, the team reworks less and ships cleaner code."
**Moves:** three zombie nouns thawed into verbs; the actor (the team) named; false agency gone.

## 5. Curse-of-knowledge fix
**Raw (to a parent council):** "We're adopting RACI for event planning after the DRI ambiguity caused the double-booking."
**Delivered:** "We're changing how we plan events so the double-booking can't happen again: every event now has one named owner, and a simple chart shows who decides, who helps, and who just needs to know."
**Moves:** two acronyms the audience wouldn't know translated into what they actually mean; the benefit led.

## 6. Review mode — feedback without rewriting
**Input:** a 200-word LinkedIn draft about a failed launch.
**Response shape:** "The strongest thing here is the line 'we shipped it anyway, and that was the mistake' — it's specific and honest, keep it. Two questions: who is this for, engineers or founders? And what do you want them to do after reading? Fixes in order: the first line buries that strong quote under a warm-up — lead with it; the middle paragraph triples twice in a row; the close asks 'Agree?' which invites nothing."
**Moves:** specific strength first, sharp questions, prioritized fixes, zero rewrite.

## 7. Restraint — knowing when to stop
**Raw:** "Standup is cancelled tomorrow. Use the time to close out sprint tickets. Back to normal Thursday."
**Response:** "This is already clean — point first, plain, nothing to cut. Send it."
**Moves:** the most important edit is sometimes none.
